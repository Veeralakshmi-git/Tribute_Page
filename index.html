Dr.A.P.J Abdul Kalam


"Missile Man Of India , President Of The Youth"

Here's the Biography of Dr. Kalam:

  • 1931-Born in Rameshwaram,India.
  • 1950- completed his schooling at the Schwartz Higher Secondary School, Ramanathapuram.
  • 1954-he graduated in physics from Saint Joseph's College, Tiruchirappalli, then affiliated with the University of Madras.
  • 1955-He moved to Madras(chennai) to study aerospace engineering in Madras Institute of Technology(MIT).
  • 1960-After graduating from the Madras Institute of Technology in 1960, Kalam joined the Aeronautical Development Establishment of the Defence Research and Development Organisation as a scientist after becoming a member of the Defence Research & Development Service.
  • 1969- Kalam was transferred to the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO).
  • 2002-Kalam became the 11th president of the Republic of India.
  • 2008-After leaving office, Kalam became a visiting professor at the IITs' (Indian Institute of Management).chancellor of the Indian Institute of Space Science and Technology Thiruvananthapuram; professor of Aerospace Engineering at Anna University and Many Other Educational Institution.
  • 2015-Died In Shillong,India.

Here's Some of the Work of Dr. Kalam:

  • 1965-Kalam had first started work on an expandable rocket project independently at DRDO.
  • 1970-Kalam directed two projects, Project Devil and Project Valiant, which sought to develop ballistic missiles from the technology of the successful SLV programme.
  • 1980-He was the project director of India's first Satellite Launch Vehicle (SLV-III) which successfully deployed the Rohini satellite.
  • 1992-1999-Kalam served as the Chief Scientific Adviser to the Prime Minister and Secretary of the Defence Research and Development Organisation.
  • 1998-along with cardiologist Soma Raju, Kalam developed a low cost coronary stent, named the "Kalam-Raju Stent"
  • 2002-became the President of India,He was also the first scientist and the first bachelor to occupy Rashtrapati Bhawan
  • 2003-Kalam supported the need of Uniform Civil Code in India.
  • 2006-signing the Office of Profit Bill was the toughest decision he had taken during his tenure.
  • 2012-Kalam launched a programme for the youth of India called the What Can I Give Movement, with a central theme of defeating corruption.

Here's Some of the Awards of Dr. Kalam:

  • 1981-Padma Bhushan by Government of India.
  • 1990-Padma Vibhushan by Government of India.
  • 1994-Distinguished Fellow by Institute of Directors (India).
  • 1995-Honorary Fellow by National Academy of Medical Sciences of india.
  • 1997-Bharat Ratna by President Of India.
  • 1998-Veer Savarkar Award by Government of India.
  • 2000-Ramanujan Award by Alwars Research Centre, Chennai.
  • 2007-King Charles II Medal, Royal Society, UK.
  • 2008-Doctor of Science by Aligarh Muslim University, Aligarh.
  • 2008-Doctor of Engineering by Nanyang Technological University, Singapore.
  • 2009-Hoover Medal by ASME Foundation, USA.
